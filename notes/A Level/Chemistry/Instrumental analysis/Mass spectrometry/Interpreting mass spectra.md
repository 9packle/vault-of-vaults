The spectrum will (essentially) show the relative molecular mass for the ions as well as their abundance, allowing us some insight into the structure of the molecule since certain isomers can split into some fragments but not others. 

Certain atoms in the molecule can also cause a cluster of peaks since elements have isotopes with different relative atomic masses:

This is the spectrum for molybdenum, which has 7 different isotopes.

The $\textup{M}+1$ peak in compounds containing carbon can be used to determine the amount of carbon atoms in the molecule since we know that there is around 1.1% $^{13}\textup{C}$, so we can use this with the binomial distribution to find out how many carbon atoms are in the molecule.
