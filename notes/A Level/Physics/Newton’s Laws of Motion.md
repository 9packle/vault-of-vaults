Newton’s first law states that an object will continue to move at a constant velocity unless there are external forces acting upon it. (This is only true in inertial frames of reference)

![[image 2.png]]

Newton’s second law states that the net force upon an object is equal to its rate of change in momentum.

![[image 3.png]]

Newton’s third law states that when object A exerts a force on object B, object B will exert a force on object A that is equal in magnitude, opposite in direction and of the same type.
