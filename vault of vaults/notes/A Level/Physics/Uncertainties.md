Absolute uncertainty is the numerical measure of uncertainty, e.g. 1000 ± 0.1g while percentage or relative uncertainty is the uncertainty represented as a ratio, e.g. 1000 ± 0.01%

A fast way to calculate uncertainties is that when adding or subtracting, the absolute uncertainties should be added while when multiplying or dividing, the relative uncertainties should be added. However it would be more accurate to simply work out a value at the maximum amount of error and simply work out how much it deviates from the value given without uncertainty.
