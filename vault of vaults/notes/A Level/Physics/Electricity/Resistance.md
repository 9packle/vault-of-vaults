The resistance of a component is the ratio of the potential difference across it to the current passing through it:
$$
R = \frac{V}{I}
$$
This implies that for a component with constant resistance, $V \propto I$.

This is exactly what Ohm's law states. In this way resistors which obey this can be referred to as ohmic resistors.

# Superconductors
[[Superconductivity - BCS Theory]]

Superconductivity is a phenomenon where the resistance of a material abruptly drops to negligible amounts below a critical temperature.

During the transition state the material also expels all external magnetic fields, making it different to a perfect conductor.

Superconductors with higher critical temperatures can be used in combination with liquid nitrogen to be used in particle accelerators and MRI machines for a fraction of the cost of a conventional superconductor since those require liquid helium, which is much more expensive.
