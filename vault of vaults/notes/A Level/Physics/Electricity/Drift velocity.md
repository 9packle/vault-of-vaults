The instantaneous drift velocity of a single electron is quite fast, but for the electrons in general it is quite slow. The equation for drift velocity is
$$
I = nAve
$$
Where $I$ is the current, $n$ is the number of free electrons per $m^3$ in the material, $A$ is the cross-sectional area of the wire, $v$ is the drift velocity and $e$ is the elementary charge.

Even though drift velocity of electrons is quite slow, the reason that a lightbulb will instantly turn on when the switch is flicked is because the electric field travels at the speed of light through the wire and the electrons in the bulb start moving (basically) instantly.
