The photoelectric effect is the phenomenon where if light of the correct frequency is shined onto a metal's surface, electrons will be emitted from the surface matching the frequency according to Planck's equation:
$$
E = hf
$$
The fact that the energy of the electron is not connected to the intensity of the light showed that this phenomenon cannot be explained with classical physics, and showed that light exhibits wave-particle duality, and that EM radiation exists in discrete packets of energy - each electron is freed by a specific frequency of photon which corresponds to the energy level of the electron.

The maximum kinetic energy of an escaping electron is given by
$$
E_k = hf - \phi
$$
where $\phi$ is the work function of the metal, which represents the minimum amount of energy required to free an electron from its surface. Therefore if the energy of a photon is given by $E = hf$, the remaining energy of the photon will go to the kinetic energy of the escaping electron.
