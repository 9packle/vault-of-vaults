In the mass spectrometer the molecules being tested are first vapourised, ionised, then accelerated and subjected to a magnetic field as they pass round a bend, causing fragments to move by varying amounts as they hit the detector around the bend - lighter fragments are bent more while heavier ones are bent less.

Only certain fragments make it to the detector for a certain strength of magnetic field:

![[Screenshot 2026-05-23 172728.png]]

In order to pick up the other fragments and ions the strength of the magnetic field is changed to allow other ions to come to the detector.

The horizontal scale is measured in $\textup{m/z}$ which is a combination of the mass and charge of the ions passing by. The vertical axis is based off the most abundant ion received, and is measured in relative abundance.
