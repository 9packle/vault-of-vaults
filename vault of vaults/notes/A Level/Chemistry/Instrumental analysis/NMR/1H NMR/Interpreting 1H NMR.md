For low resolution spectra only the integrals and chemical shifts can be used, and this can be done in the same way as in $^{13}\textup{C}$ NMR:

[[Interpreting 13C NMR]]

For high resolution spectra the peak splitting can be seen, with the splitting being determined by the number of adjacent hydrogens in different hydrogen environments.

![[Screenshot 2026-05-23 131054.png]]

To identify an $-\textup{OH}$ group it is sometimes possible to add a few drops of $\textup{D}_2 \textup{O}$ so that the $\textup{H}$ in the group is replaced by a $\textup{D}$ which does not show up in the spectrum. Then the two spectra can be compared and the peak or peaks which disappear are the $-\textup{OH}$ groups. $-\textup{OH}$ groups also do not split most of the time since there are no adjacent hydrogens.
