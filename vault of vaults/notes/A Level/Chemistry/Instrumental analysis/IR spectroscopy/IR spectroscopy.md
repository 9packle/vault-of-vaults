IR spectroscopy uses the fact that organic compounds have bonds which will absorb regions of IR frequencies and stretch or bend, allowing a detector to check which frequencies have been partially absorbed and therefore detect which bonds and functional groups are present in an organic compound.

[[Interpreting IR spectra]]

The vertical scale is measured in % transmittance of the IR, and the horizontal scale is measured in wavenumbers ($\textup{cm}^{-1}$), which is calculated by $\frac{1}{\lambda}$ with $\lambda$ being the wavelength of IR absorbed.
