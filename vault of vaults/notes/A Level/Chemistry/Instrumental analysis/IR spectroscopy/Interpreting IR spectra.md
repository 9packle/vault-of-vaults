The IR spectra of molecules will have absorption peaks at certain wavenumbers which correspond to bonds in the molecule:

![[Screenshot 2026-05-23 171005.png]]

The region from about 1500 to 500 $\textup{cm}^{-1}$ is known as the fingerprint region which can be used as a unique region which can be matched with a database to identify certain molecule. The individual peaks in this region can be very hard to pick out and identify, but the pattern in the region is unique to each molecule.

This also means that when a new molecule is made, its fingerprint region can be registered in a database to allow the molecule to be identified in the future.
