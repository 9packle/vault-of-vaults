Acyl chlorides can react with more reactive phenols (such as phenol itself) at room temperature to form the phenyl ester:

![[Screenshot 2026-05-25 122553.png]]

This reaction can also be improved by first reacting the phenol with $\textup{NaOH}$ as the phenoxide ion reacts much faster:

![[Screenshot 2026-05-25 122800.png]]

The reaction with acid anhydrides is also similar, with the reaction with phenol at room temperature being much slower than the reaction with the phenoxide ion. Either a carboxylic acid or the carboxylate salt is produced as the side product.
