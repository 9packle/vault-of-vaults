Aromatic compounds are those with one or more benzene rings.

Initially the structure for benzene was a confused subject since it doesn’t exhibit any of the typical characteristics of unsaturated compounds - it doesn’t decolourise bromine water or form addition compounds. Then Kekulé came up with a structure:

This fixed some issues with previous structures but still failed to explain how the energy taken to hydrogenate benzene to cyclohexane was so much higher than expected. This was subsequently fixed by the resonance structure which described the six-membered ring as having a delocalised ring of electrons
