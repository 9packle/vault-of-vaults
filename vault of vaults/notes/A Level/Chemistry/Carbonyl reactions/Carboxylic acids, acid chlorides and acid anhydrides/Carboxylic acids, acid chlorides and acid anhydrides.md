Carboxylic acids contain a $-\textup{COOH}$ functional group. They are acidic because the hydrogen in the group can be lost to form the carboxylate salt.

In a pure carboxylic acid hydrogen bonding can occur between a pair of molecules making the intermolecular forces in it much stronger. This ability to form hydrogen bonds also allows them to dissolve in water for carboxylic acids with less than four carbons.

Acyl (or acid) chlorides contain a $-\textup{COCl}$ functional group which can be produced from reacting a carboxylic acid with $\textup{SOCl}_2$.

Acyl chlorides are much more reactive and cannot be said to dissolve in water since they react violently in water.
