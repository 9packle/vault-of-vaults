Alcohols can be classified using how many alkyl groups are bonded to the carbon with the $-\textup{OH}$ group: a primary alcohol has one group attached, a secondary alcohol has two and a tertiary has three.

The $-\textup{OH}$ group can participate in hydrogen bonding, and the strength of the hydrogen bond decreases with the length of the alkyl chain attached as a larger part of the molecule resembles a hydrocarbon.
