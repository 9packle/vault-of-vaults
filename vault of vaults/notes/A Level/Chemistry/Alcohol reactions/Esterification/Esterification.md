Alcohols can react with carboxylic acids or its derivatives to form an ester:

![[Screenshot 2026-05-25 120149.png]]

The conditions required with carboxylic acids are heat and concentrated $\textup{H}_2 \textup{SO}_4$. The reaction with carboxylic acids is slow and reversible.

With acyl chlorides the reaction is not reversible and will occur at room temperature. 

With acid anhydrides gentle heating is required.

[[Reactions of carboxylic acids & derivatives]]

Phenols will not react with carboxylic acids to form a phenyl ester, but will with acyl chlorides and acid anhydrides:

[[Esterification with acyl chlorides and acid anhydrides]]

This is because carboxylic acids are not reactive enough, since the electrons in p-orbitals on the oxygen can join the conjugated $\pi$ system to make phenol more stable.
