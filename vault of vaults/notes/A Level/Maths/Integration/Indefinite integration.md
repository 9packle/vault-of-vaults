Integration is the counterpart of differentiation. Integrating an expression will take it back to $y$ from $\frac{dy}{dx}$.

Finding the integral of a function $f(x)$ is written as 

$$
\int f(x)\hspace{1mm}dx
$$

The $\int$ means *the integral of* and $dx$ means *with respect to x*.

The Fundamental theorem of calculus states that 

$$
\int f(x)\hspace{1mm}dx = F(x) + C \Longleftrightarrow f(x) = \frac{d}{dx}(F(x))
$$
