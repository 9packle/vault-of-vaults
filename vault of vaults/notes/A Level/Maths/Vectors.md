Scalar quantities only have a magnitude, while vectors have both magnitude and direction.

Vectors are drawn as lines with arrowheads on them to show their direction.

They are written with a lowercase bold letter or a lowercase underlined letter.

Vectors can be added by drawing the arrows nose to tail. The single vector which goes from the start to the end of the combined vectors is called the resultant vector.

## Scalar multiplication

You can multiply a vector by a scalar. The length changes and the direction depends on if the scalar is positive or negative. This also means that if you can write a vector as a multiple of another, the two vectors are parallel or collinear. The vectors $\overrightarrow{AB}$ and $\overrightarrow{BC}$ are collinear if $\overrightarrow{AB}$ is a multiple of $\overrightarrow{BC}$, for example, as they share a point and are parallel, so they must be collinear.

## $i$ + $j$ units

The vectors $i$ and $j$ are unit vectors, of the positive $x$-axis and $y$-axis respectively. 

Any vector in two dimensions can be expressed as a sum of $i$ and $j$ vectors

## Column vectors

Column vectors are another way of expressing vectors. You can simply write the $i$ component of a vector on top and the $j$ component on the bottom like so: 

$$
\begin{pmatrix} i\\ j\\ \end{pmatrix}
$$
