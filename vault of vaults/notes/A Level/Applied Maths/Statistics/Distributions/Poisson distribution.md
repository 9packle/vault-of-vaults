The poisson distribution can be written as
$$
X \sim Po(\lambda)
$$
where $\lambda$ is the average constant rate that an event occurs. The probability can be found by
$$
P(X = x) = \frac{e^{\lambda}\lambda^{-x}}{x!}
$$
For events to be modelled by the poisson distribution they must be independent, occur singly, and have a constant average rate of occurring.

The mean and variance can be calculated by
$$
E(X) = Var(X) = \lambda
$$
