Natural numbers consist of $1, 2, 3, 4, 5,  ..., n, ...$ and the set of such numbers is denoted by $\mathbb{N}$.

Integers consist of $0, ±1, ±2, ±3, ±4, ..., ±n, ...$ and the set of such numbers is denoted by $\mathbb{Z}$

### Linear Diophantine equations

The equation $ax + by = n$ has integer solutions if, and only if, (a, b) is a factor of n.
